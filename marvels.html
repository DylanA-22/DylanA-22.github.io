The Marvels 1/8/24

The Marvels, featuring Brie Larson as Captain Marvel, is a tremendously entertaining film with stunning cinematography. Despite the public uproar, I, like many other moviegoers, thoroughly liked the film. The tone of the film is markedly different from that of Captain Marvel. There were several occasions when the entire theater laughed at a pun or joke. This is due in part to the performance and magnetism of Iman Vellani (Ms. Marvel). In her second appearance as Ms. Marvel, she performs an excellent job of assisting Captain Marvel on her adventure across space while also providing comedic relief. Monica Rambeau (Teyonah Parris) also plays an important role in the film, particularly at the climax.Other supporting characters, including Nick Fury (Samuel L. Jackson) and the entire Ms. Marvel family, play their parts flawlessly and add to the film's enjoyment. The only drawbacks are the length of the film and the villain. The film is only an hour and 45 minutes long, which is short in comparison to the other MCU films. When the film concluded, I found myself wondering, "that's it?" because I wanted to see more. Dar-Benn (Zawe Ashton), the villain, was also forgettable. I was not invested in her development and felt no empathy for her story throughout the film. Despite its flaws, The Marvels is a vast improvement over Captain Marvel. This trio of female superheroes has piqued my interest in the MCU's future and has me eager to see where they will slot into the new Avengers roster. It's humorous and engaging, while also laying the groundwork for the MCU's future with the ending and post-credit scenes.
